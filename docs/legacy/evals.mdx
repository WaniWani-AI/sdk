<Warning>
`@waniwani/sdk/evals` and `@waniwani/sdk/evals/scorers` were removed entirely in this revision. The exports (`chat`, `conversation`, `loadScenarios`, `replayScenario`, `saveScenario`, `calledExpectedTool`, `hasOutput`, `toolInputFieldsMatch`, `FaqAccuracy`, `OutputFactuality`, `SafetyCheck`, `parseTaskOutput`, `createLocalReporter`) are no longer available.
</Warning>

## Why removed

The evals surface was experimental, never reached a stable shape, and depended on the now-removed chat-server catch-all. Keeping it shipped would force the chat-server adapters to stay first-class indefinitely.

## What used to live here

- **`chat`, `conversation`, `replayScenario`** — drove a multi-turn chat eval by hitting a self-hosted `/api/waniwani` endpoint
- **`loadScenarios`, `saveScenario`** — filesystem-based scenario fixtures
- **Scorers** (`calledExpectedTool`, `hasOutput`, `toolInputFieldsMatch`, `FaqAccuracy`, `OutputFactuality`, `SafetyCheck`) — Braintrust-compatible scoring helpers
- **`createLocalReporter`** — local terminal output for eval runs

## If you depended on this

You have a few options depending on what you were using:

- **For test-driven eval runs against an MCP server**: use [Braintrust](https://braintrust.dev) directly with their MCP integration, or build your own test harness against your `/mcp` endpoint with `@modelcontextprotocol/sdk`.
- **For scenario fixtures**: the JSON shape was just `{ name, turns: [{ role, content }] }`. Keep your fixtures and load them with your own loader.
- **For tool-call scorers**: the implementations were thin wrappers over Braintrust's `autoevals`. Use `autoevals` directly.

## Cross-repo migration

The following internal projects had imports from `@waniwani/sdk/evals`:

- `app/src/lib/evals/execute-eval-run.ts`
- `app/src/lib/evals/evaluators.ts`
- `app/src/app/api/mcp/projects/[id]/evaluators/[evaluatorId]/test/route.ts`
- `app/src/app/api/mcp/scenarios/[scenarioId]/replay/route.ts`
- `managed/lassie/evals/index.ts`

These need to be migrated off `@waniwani/sdk/evals` (either by pinning to a pre-removal SDK version, or replacing the imports with direct Braintrust / custom logic) before they can pick up the new SDK version.
